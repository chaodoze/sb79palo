About

About this site

An independent, educational portal about California SB 79, with a focus on Palo Alto. Phase one is a learning hub and a 5-minute simulator. The longer-term plan is ongoing coverage as the law plays out.

Who runs this

A Palo Alto resident who wanted to actually understand SB 79 — and help neighbors do the same. YIMBY-leaning, but committed to fair, sourced framing.

What this site is, and isn't

  • Is: a plain-English explainer, a local impact summary, and an interactive simulator.
  • Isn't: legal advice, a lobbying organization, or an official source. For binding interpretations of SB 79, consult the bill text or a land-use attorney.

How the simulator's numbers work

The simulator uses the same per-tier height and density floors that SB 79 establishes (95/75/65 ft and 160/120/100 units/acre). Parcel sizes and current uses around the University Avenue Caltrain station are approximate, drawn for clarity rather than legal precision. "Vibrancy" and "carbon" meters are deliberately rough heuristics — directional, not predictive.

The 1,600-unit completion target matches Palo Alto staff's own 25-year projection for the downtown station area.
