California Senate Bill 79 — signed Oct 10, 2025

More homes, near transit. A plain-English guide to SB 79 — and what it means for Palo Alto.

SB 79 lets housing be built taller and denser near California's busiest train and rapid-transit stops. The Palo Alto Caltrain station is one of them. This site explains the law, shows the local impact, and lets you try it for yourself.

What is SB 79, in one breath?

SB 79 is a California state law (authored by Sen. Scott Wiener, signed by Gov. Newsom on October 10, 2025) that sets minimum height and density allowances for housing near major transit stops. Cities can no longer enforce stricter local zoning that blocks midrise apartments within a half-mile of a Tier 1 station like Palo Alto Caltrain.

Translation: a 7-story apartment building next to the University Avenue Caltrain station is now legal by right — even if Palo Alto's downtown zoning today caps buildings at 50 feet.
