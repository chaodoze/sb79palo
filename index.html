California Senate Bill 79 · in effect July 1, 2026

More homes, near transit. What SB 79 does — and what it means for Palo Alto.

California passed a new law (SB 79) that lets developers build taller, denser apartment buildings within a half-mile walk of major train stations — including all three Caltrain stations in Palo Alto. The new rules kick in automatically on July 1, 2026. Cities can shape some details, but they can't say no.

SB 79 in one breath

Signed by Gov. Newsom on October 10, 2025 (authored by Sen. Scott Wiener), SB 79 requires every California city to allow taller, denser apartment buildings near major train stations. Palo Alto can't block projects that meet the new rules. The University Avenue, California Avenue, and San Antonio Caltrain stations are all covered.

Concrete example: today, Palo Alto caps buildings near the University Avenue Caltrain station at around 50 ft. After July 1, 2026, a 7-story apartment building right next to that station is allowed automatically — no Council vote, no public hearing as long as the project meets the new rules. All three Palo Alto Caltrain stations qualify at the top level (each gets more than 72 trains every weekday — we did the math).
